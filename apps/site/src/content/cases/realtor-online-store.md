title: "Realtor.com — Online Store"
company: "Realtor.com — Online Store"
role: Senior Product Designer
period: "Oct 2024 – Dec 2024"
order: 2
problem: "The Online Store — the B2B product enabling real estate agents to purchase leads and manage listings — ran on an abandoned Salesforce platform. Mobile drop-off was approximately 90%, adoption was critically low, there was no dedicated team, and the product was visually and technically disconnected from the rest of Realtor.com's ecosystem."
approach: "Worked alongside the design manager (who brought the initial proposal) and a senior manager to scope the problem before any design started. Ran heuristic analysis and structured user interviews. Research surfaced quick-win pain points for the first phase — and I deliberately prioritized fixes that also aligned the platform's components and patterns with Haven, Realtor.com's design system. The goal: short-term improvements that doubled as migration groundwork, so the eventual full transition away from Salesforce would be significantly less disruptive. Together we defined a 6-month roadmap. Led design from findings through delivery."
outcome: "Mobile drop-off cut from approximately 90% to under 10%. The roadmap justified investment — Realtor.com expanded the team, hired to grow the product. I was promoted to lead design on UpNest as a direct result."
tradeoffs: "There was no pre-existing team or budget — the work had to justify its own investment. That meant ruthless prioritization: fix the high-pain, low-effort issues first to demonstrate value, while making sure those fixes also moved the product closer to Haven's design language. Not every improvement was purely about UX — some decisions were architectural bets on the migration that was coming. Some improvements were intentionally deferred to give the new team their own problems to solve."
