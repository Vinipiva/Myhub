title: PepsiCo
company: PepsiCo
role: Senior Product Designer
period: "Nov 2025 – Mar 2026"
order: 1
problem: "Enterprise Power BI dashboards across PepsiCo's global teams had inconsistent KPI visualization patterns, causing interpretation friction and low adoption. Decision-makers across regions were reading the same data differently — slowing reporting cycles and generating avoidable support tickets."
approach: "Embedded into PepsiCo's cross-functional environment — working alongside product, data engineering, and global operations teams across regions. Standardized layout patterns, interaction logic, and data storytelling principles. Built foundational design guardrails to ensure consistency across internal analytics tools, regardless of team or reporting domain."
outcome: "Dashboard adoption up 23%. Support tickets down 41%. Design guardrails adopted across teams as the standard for enterprise analytics UX — reducing friction between data and decision-makers at scale."
tradeoffs: "Working in a highly matrixed enterprise means design decisions require broader alignment than in a typical product team. The tradeoff was speed vs. consistency: moving faster would mean local solutions that couldn't scale. Choosing to invest in shared guardrails upfront took longer but produced something every team could build on."
